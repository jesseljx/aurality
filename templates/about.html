User guide

Use our Search Page to filter and search for songs.

How to search:

  1. Navigate to the Search Page
  2. Use the dropdown filters to set min/max filter values
  3. Type in the search bar
  4. Change the searchbar's song/artist dropdown to search by either song name or artist name
  5. Press Apply Filters, the search icon, or the enter key to search

Notes:

  • The filters and the search bar narrow your search, but using both are not necessary.
  • The Search Page displays total matching results, but only loads the top 20,000 search results accessible by the Load more button.

Tip: Use your browser's back button to return, then edit your previous searches.

Our Dataset and Filter Descriptions

Our dataset (sourced from Kaggle) includes over 200,000 songs released from 2000 to 2023, encompassing a variety of genres and artists.

Below are the parameters in the dataset, which you can filter on the Search Page:

  • Genre

    Genres in our dataset: alt-rock, dance, dancehall, detroit-techno, edm, electro, electronic, hard-rock, indie-pop, k-pop, minimal-techno, pop, power-pop, psych-rock, punk-rock, rock, rock-n-roll.

  • Key

    Keys in our dataset: C, C#/Db, D, D#/Eb, E, F, F#/Gb, G, G#/Ab, A, A#/Bb, B.

  • Mode

    Modes in our dataset: Major and minor.

  • Tempo (BPM)

    Speed of the song in beats per minute (BPM). A common tempo is 120 BPM.

  • Loudness (dB)

    Measured in decibels (dB). Max loudness is 3.313 dB, but most values are negative.

  • Duration (ms)

    Song length in milliseconds (ms). A typical song is about 4 minutes or 240,000 ms.

  • Popularity

    Scale from 0 to 100. Based on Spotify user engagement, but exact calculation is unclear (source).

  • Danceability

    Measures how suitable a song is for dancing on a 0 to 1 scale.

  • Energy

    Indicates a song's intensity and activity level on a 0 to 1 scale.

  • Valence

    Measures musical positiveness on a 0 to 1 scale. Tracks with high valence sound more positive (e.g. happy, cheerful, euphoric), while tracks with low valence sound more negative (e.g. sad, depressed, angry).

  • Year

    Year the song was released, ranging from 2000 to 2023.


For more information on dataset attributes, visit the Kaggle dataset.
